Chicken-Bacon-Ranch Pizza Sticks

to find more info.... Click here .

Ingredients Directions
1 can Pillsbury™ refrigerated classic pizza crust Heat oven to 400°F. Lightly grease cookie sheet with shortening or cooking spray.
1/3 cup ranch dressing Unroll pizza crust onto cookie sheet; press dough out into 13x10-inch rectangle. Spread dressing over dough. Sprinkle chicken, bacon and half of the green onions over dressing in even layers. Top with cheese.
2 cups shredded cooked chicken breast Bake 12 to 15 minutes or until crust is lightly browned and cheese in center is bubbly.
1/3 cup cooked crumbled bacon (about 4 slices) Sprinkle with remaining half of green onions. Cool slightly. Cut into about 1 1/2-inch-wide strips. Serve with additional dressing for dipping.
1/2 cup chopped green onions (8 medium)
2 cups shredded Cheddar-Monterey Jack cheese blend (8 oz)

Beef Tortilla Taco Casserole

to find more info.... Click here .

Ingredients Directions
1 lb lean (at least 80%) ground beef Heat oven to 350°F. In 12-inch skillet, cook beef over medium-high heat 5 to 7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thoroughly cooked; drain. Stir in taco seasoning mix, water and bell pepper strips. Simmer 3 to 4 minutes, stirring frequently, until thickened. Stir in salsa and corn.
1 package (1 oz) Old El Paso™ 25% less sodium taco seasoning mix Spray 13x9-inch (3-quart) baking dish with cooking spray. Cut tortillas in half; place 6 tortilla halves in bottom of baking dish.
2/3 cup water Top with half of beef mixture (about 3 1/2 cups). Sprinkle with 3/4 cup of the cheese and remaining tortilla halves. Top with remaining beef mixture and cheese.
1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch strips Spray piece of foil large enough to cover baking dish with cooking spray. Cover baking dish with foil sprayed side down. Bake 35 to 40 minutes or until heated through and cheese is melted.
2 3/4 cups Old El Paso™ Thick 'n Chunky salsa To Make Ahead:Wrap foil-covered casserole in double layer of plastic wrap; label and freeze up to 3 months. To thaw and bake:Thaw overnight or until completely thawed in refrigerator. Remove plastic wrap. Bake 1 hour to 1 hour 10 minutes or until center is hot (165°F) and cheese is melted. To bake from freezer (no thawing):Heat oven to 350°F. Remove plastic. Bake 1 hour 30 minutes to 1 hour 40 minutes or until center is hot (165°F) and cheese is melted.
1 package (12 oz) frozen whole kernel sweet corn
6 Old El Paso™ flour tortillas for soft tacos & fajitas (6 inch)
2 cups shredded Colby-Monterey Jack cheese blend (8 oz)

Cheese sliders

ingredients Directions
24 slices of deli honey ham Cut rolls in half and spread mayo onto 1 side of the rolls. Place a slice or two of ham and slice of swiss cheese in roll. Replace the top of the rolls and bunch them closely together into a baking dish.
6 Slices of swiss cheese, cut into fourths In a medium bowl, whisk together poppy seeds, dijon mustard, melted butter, onion powder and worcestershire sauce.
1/3 cup mayonnaise Pour sauce over the rolls, just covering the tops. Cover with foil and let sit for 10 minutes.
1 tablespoon poppy seeds Bake at 350 degrees for 10 minutes or until cheese is melted. Uncover and cook for additional 2 minutes until tops are slightly browned and crisp. Serve warm.
1 1/2 tablespoons dijon mustard
1/2 cup butter melted
1 tablespoon Onion Powder
1/2 teaspoon worcestershire sauce
